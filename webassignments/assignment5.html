Challenge 5 - Accessibility Statement

As with everything in our modern world, accessibility is always something that we should strive towards in creating websites. In this particular assignment, there were a few areas that could be improved upon to make them more accessible. For example, the chessboard exercise is complete, but the pieces could be made more distinct from one another for people who are visually impaired. One could say the same about the coloring of the Google search page. The dark purple with the black background is not the easiest thing to read. Overall, these challenges could definitely be improved.

What changes did I make?

In Challenge 2, I edited the chessboard to have bigger pieces and better sizing, per the comments to my last assignment. I realized that the two tables on the page (the chessboard and the Google search page) were trying to use the same style elements, so I renamed the classes so they could have different font sizes.

In Challenge 4, I changed the font to a sans serif font, to make it look more like a Google page. I tried to use product sans, which is the actual Google font, but I couldn't get it to work.

The new tag I used was the del tag, which I implemented on Challenge 3. I found it on this website.

I didn't make any changes to Challenges 1 or 5, because there were no comments on my previous assignment.
